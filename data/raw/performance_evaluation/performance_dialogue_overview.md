# Performance Dialogue at McGill University

## What is Performance Dialogue?

Performance Dialogue (PD) is a **documented and time-sensitive conversation** between a supervisor and an employee that ensures organizational effectiveness and staff engagement. It is McGill's primary framework for performance management.

The conversation provides an opportunity to:

- Identify work objectives and evolving priorities
- Explore developmental and training options
- Recognize successes and determine areas for improvement
- Discuss career aspirations

## When Does It Take Place?

Performance Dialogue is conducted on an **annual cycle**. The reference period runs over the course of the preceding year. Employees and supervisors are expected to complete the process within the window set by Organizational Development (OD).

## Process Overview

1. **Employee preparation**: The employee fills out the Performance Dialogue and Appraisal Form first, reflecting on objectives, competencies, and career aspirations.
2. **Submission to supervisor**: The completed form is sent to the supervisor before the session as preparation material.
3. **Performance Dialogue session**: The supervisor and employee meet to have the documented conversation.
4. **Form submission**: The completed form is submitted through the appropriate administrative channel.

Recommendation from OD: Having employees fill out the forms first, then send to the supervisor, results in a more productive and equitable conversation.

## Key Components

| Component                                         | Purpose                                                                                                                      |
| ------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Unit Objectives**                               | Identify the current needs of the unit                                                                                       |
| **Individual Work Objectives**                    | Define day-to-day contributions that meet unit needs; must be SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) |
| **Behavioural Competencies**                      | Assess "soft" skills that support achieving objectives; identify areas for growth (see Behavioural Competencies policy)      |
| **Training and Development**                      | Plan how behavioural competencies and technical skills will be developed to meet current and future objectives               |
| **Career Aspirations and Associated Development** | Discuss where the employee wants to be in 3–5 years and what development supports that path                                  |

## Link to Merit

Performance Dialogue results are considered in merit decisions. Strong performance documentation through PD supports salary progression reviews.

## Responsibility

Both the **employee and the supervisor** share responsibility for Performance Dialogue. In cases where two supervisors share equal supervision of one employee, they must coordinate to complete a single PD process together.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Waiting until the last minute rather than treating it as an ongoing process
- Focusing only on recent events rather than the full reference period
- Treating it as a one-way review rather than a genuine two-way dialogue
- Skipping the self-assessment step for employees
