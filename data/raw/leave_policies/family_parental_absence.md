# Family and Parental Absence Policy

## Scope

This policy applies to all regular non-unionized administrative and support staff, with the exception of University Executives ("E").

---

## Definitions

**Relative** means, in addition to the employee's spouse: the child, father, mother, brother, sister, and grandparents of the employee or the employee's spouse, as well as those persons' spouses, their children, and their children's spouses. The following are also considered relatives:

- A person who has acted or is acting as a foster family for the employee or employee's spouse
- A child for whom the employee or employee's spouse has acted or is acting as a foster family
- A tutor, curator, or mandatary of or for the employee or employee's spouse
- Any person for whom the employee is entitled to benefits under an Act for care provided owing to the person's state of health

**Spouse** means either of two persons who are married/civil union and cohabiting, or living in a de facto union for one year or more, or are parents of the same child.

---

## Short-Term Family Absence (Up to 10 Days/Year)

- An employee may be absent **without pay** for up to **10 days per year** to fulfill obligations relating to:
  - The care, health, or education of the employee's child or the child of the employee's spouse
  - The state of health of a relative or a person for whom the employee acts as a caregiver (as attested by a health professional governed by the Professional Code)
- This leave may be divided into individual days, or a day may be split with University consent.

---

## Extended Family Absence (Up to 16 Weeks)

- An employee may be absent **without pay** for up to **16 weeks over 12 months** when they must stay with a relative or caregiver recipient who has a **serious illness or serious accident** (attested by a health professional).
- If the relative is a **minor child**, the maximum absence is **36 weeks over 12 months**.

---

## Extended Absence for a Child with Critical Illness

- If a minor child has a **serious and potentially mortal illness** (attested by a medical certificate), the employee is entitled to an extension of the above absence ending **no later than 104 weeks** after the beginning of the absence.

---

## Benefits During Unpaid Family Absence

- The University continues to pay its share of benefit plan costs for plans the employee chooses to continue, provided the employee makes their regular contributions.

---

## Return to Work

- Upon return, the employee is reinstated in the position held at the start of the unpaid leave.
- If the position has been abolished, the employee is accorded all rights and privileges that would have been granted had they been at work.

---

## Procedure

- The employee must advise the University of their absence **as soon as possible**.
- The employee must take **reasonable steps to limit the duration** of the leave.
- The University may request documentation attesting the reasons for the absence.
- Family/parental absence will not be granted if it coincides with vacation or another University leave.
