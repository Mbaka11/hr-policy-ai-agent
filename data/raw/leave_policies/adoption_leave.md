# Adoption Leave Policy

## Scope

This policy applies to all regular non-unionized non-academic staff, with the exception of University Executives ("E"). Temporary employees are also covered but are not eligible for Extended Parental Leave.

---

## Procedures

- Employees who legally adopt a child are entitled to a maximum of **five (5) consecutive weeks** of adoption leave. This period is included within the 65-week parental leave period.
- Employees receiving QPIP indemnities receive **95% of regular salary** (minus QPIP benefits and normal deductions) for up to 5 weeks.
- All benefits continue during adoption leave.
- Leave must be requested via **Workday** with written notice to the supervisor, supported by documentation evidencing the fact of adoption.
- Leave begins in the week the child is placed under the employee's responsibility, or at any other time agreed with the University.
- **Vacation continues to accrue** during adoption leave.
- Employees traveling outside Quebec to adopt are entitled to an unpaid leave of up to **10 weeks**, with at least **2 weeks' prior notice** to the supervisor.

---

## Return to Work

- The University sends written notice of leave expiry during the **4th week before expiry**.
- Failure to return at expiry results in an automatic **4-week unpaid extension**; failure to return after that is deemed resignation.
- Upon return, the employee is reinstated in their former position. If the position was abolished, Employment Security provisions apply.
- Salary upon return is adjusted for any increases implemented during the leave.
