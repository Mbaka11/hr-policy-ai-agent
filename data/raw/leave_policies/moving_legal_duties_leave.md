# Moving and Legal Duties Leave Policy

## Scope

This policy applies to all regular non-unionized administrative and support staff, with the exception of University Executives ("E").

---

## Moving Leave

- An employee is entitled to **one (1) day of paid leave per reference year** for the purpose of moving to a new permanent residence.

---

## Legal Duties Leave

- An employee will be granted **paid leave** if called to perform **jury duty** or to act as a **witness in a legal proceeding in which they are not a party**.
  - The employee must remit to the University any pay received for fulfilling these duties.
  - If the pay received exceeds their regular salary, the University will reimburse the difference.
- An employee called to act as a **witness in a legal proceeding related to their University duties** (in which they are not a party) will be granted paid leave, and paid at the **overtime rate** for any time required outside regular working hours.
- If an employee's presence is required in a **civil, administrative, or penal court as a party** in a proceeding, they may use accumulated vacation, accumulated overtime, personal leave, or apply for unpaid leave.

---

## Procedure

The employee must advise the University of their absence as soon as possible. The University may request a document attesting the reasons for the absence.

**Exception:** Social leave for legal duties may be granted even if it coincides with another University leave, when the employee is called as a witness in a proceeding pertaining to University business.
