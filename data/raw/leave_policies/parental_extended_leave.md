# Parental Leave and Extended Parental Leave Policy

## Purpose

To enable employees to care for and bond with a newborn or newly adopted child.

## Scope

This policy applies to all regular non-unionized non-academic staff, with the exception of University Executives ("E"). Temporary employees are covered but are **not eligible for Extended Parental Leave**.

---

## Entitlement

- Parents of a newborn or adopted child are entitled to **parental leave without pay of up to 65 weeks**, ending no later than **78 weeks** after birth or adoption.
- Following parental leave, an **extended parental leave** without pay is available, ending no later than **104 weeks** after the end of maternity, paternity, or adoption leave.
- Employees who adopt a spouse's child are also entitled to parental and extended parental leaves.

---

## Benefits During Leave

Employees wishing to continue benefit plans must notify the University **in writing before leave commences**:

- During **parental leave**: both the employee and the University make regular contributions.
- During **extended parental leave**: the employee pays the **full cost** of continued benefits.

---

## Vacation and Service Credit

- Service credit for vacation accrual applies **only up to the end of the 65-week parental leave**. Credit does not accumulate beyond that.
- **Vacation does not accrue** during parental or extended parental leave.
- Employees on **part-time parental leave** receive service credit up to the end of week 65.

---

## Part-Time Extended Parental Leave

- A part-time option is available (minimum **14 hours/week**, up to **2.5 days/week**, for up to **104 weeks** after birth or adoption).
- The employee must stipulate the leave arrangement in writing. In case of disagreement with the University, the employee has the right to up to 2.5 days per week.
- Employees may change between full-time and part-time extended parental leave **once**, with at least **30 days' written notice**.

---

## Return to Work

- The University sends written notice of leave expiry in the **4th week before** it expires.
- The employee must give **2 weeks' written notice** before returning. Failure to do so or failure to return is deemed resignation.
- The employee may return early with **30 days' written notice**.
- Upon return, the employee is reinstated in the position held before the original maternity, paternity, or adoption leave. If the position was abolished, Employment Security provisions apply.
- Salary upon return is adjusted for any increases implemented during the leave.
