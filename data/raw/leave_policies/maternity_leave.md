# Maternity Leave Policy

## Purpose

To enable the employee to care for and bond with a newborn child.

## Scope

This policy applies to all regular non-unionized non-academic staff, with the exception of University Executives ("E"). Temporary employees are also covered.

---

## Procedures

- An eligible employee may take a maternity leave of up to **twenty (20) consecutive weeks**. The earliest start date is sixteen (16) weeks prior to the expected delivery date.
- The employee must specify intended leave dates **in writing** to their supervisor at least **two (2) weeks** prior to commencement, along with a medical certificate confirming pregnancy and expected delivery date.
- A medical leave from pregnancy termination before the 20th week is treated as **fully paid sick leave**.
- In the event of termination of pregnancy at or after the 20th week, maternity leave commences immediately and totals 20 weeks.
- Complications of pregnancy resulting in medical leave are treated as fully paid sick leave until delivery, at which point maternity leave begins.
- If working conditions present physical dangers or infection risks, the University will attempt to relocate the employee. If unable, the employee receives **special paid leave** until relocation is possible or until delivery.
- If a medical certificate attests that the employee's health or the child's health requires an extension, maternity leave will be extended accordingly (no indemnity or salary during extension).
- If the newborn cannot leave the hospital or is hospitalized within 15 days of birth, the employee may interrupt the maternity leave and return to work (one interruption only).
- An employee who gives birth prematurely and whose child is hospitalized may also interrupt maternity leave and resume it when the child no longer requires hospitalization.
- Absences for certified medical appointments related to pregnancy are considered **paid leave**.

---

## Indemnities

- Employees receiving QPIP indemnities are entitled to an indemnity equal to **95% of regular salary** (minus QPIP benefits and normal payroll deductions) until the end of week 20.
- Employees with at least 20 weeks of service who are **not eligible for QPIP** receive an indemnity equal to their regular salary (minus normal deductions) until the end of week 10.
- All contributory benefit plans continue while the employee receives maternity indemnity.
- Indemnities are adjusted for any salary increases during the leave.
- Employees receive credit for continuous service during maternity leave for the purposes of Employment Security and Vacation policies. **Vacation continues to accrue during maternity leave.**

---

## Return to Work

- In the 4th week before expiry of maternity leave, the University sends written notice of the expiry date.
- The employee must give **written notice of return at least 2 weeks** before returning.
- Failure to return results in an automatic **4-week unpaid extension**; failure to return after that extension is deemed resignation.
- Employees returning within 2 weeks of delivery must provide a medical certificate attesting fitness to work.
- Upon return, the employee is reinstated in their former position. If the position was abolished, Employment Security provisions apply.
