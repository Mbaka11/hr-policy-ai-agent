# Bereavement Leave Policy

## Scope

This policy applies to all regular non-unionized administrative and support staff, with the exception of University Executives ("E").

---

## Definitions

**Relative** means, in addition to the employee's spouse: the child, father, mother, brother, sister, and grandparents of the employee or the employee's spouse, as well as those persons' spouses, their children, and their children's spouses (including the employee's grandchildren). The following are also considered relatives:

- A person who has acted or is acting as a foster family for the employee or employee's spouse
- A child for whom the employee or employee's spouse has acted or is acting as a foster family
- A tutor or curator of the employee or employee's spouse, or a person under their tutorship or curatorship
- An incapable person who has designated the employee or employee's spouse as mandatary
- Any person for whom the employee is entitled to benefits under an Act for the assistance and care provided owing to the person's state of health

**Spouse** means either of two persons who:

- Are married or in a civil union and cohabiting
- Are of opposite or same sex, living together in a de facto union, and are the parents of the same child
- Are of opposite or same sex and have been living together in a de facto union for **one year or more**

---

## Entitlements

All bereavement leave is granted **without loss of regular salary**.

| Relationship                                                           | Entitlement                |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Spouse or child (or child of spouse)                                   | 5 consecutive working days |
| Mother, father, or mother/father of spouse                             | 3 consecutive working days |
| Brother, sister, or brother/sister of spouse                           | 3 consecutive working days |
| Grandparent or grandchild                                              | 2 consecutive working days |
| Aunt, uncle, nephew, niece, daughter/son-in-law, sister/brother-in-law | 1 working day              |

- **One (1) additional day** is granted if the employee must travel more than **160 km** from their residence to attend the funeral.

---

## Extended Bereavement Leave

In the event of the death of a spouse, child, father, mother, brother, sister, or the spouse's child, father, mother, brother, or sister, the employee may additionally take:

- Unpaid leave, accumulated vacation, and/or accumulated overtime
- **Not to exceed fifteen (15) working days** in total

---

## Procedure

The employee must advise the University of their absence as soon as possible. The University may request a document attesting the reasons for the absence.

Social leaves (including bereavement) will not be granted if they coincide with vacation or another University leave.
