# Unpaid Leave of Absence Policy

## Scope

This policy applies to all regular non-unionized non-academic staff, with the exception of University Executives ("E"). "Regular" refers to employees who have successfully completed their probationary period.

---

## Policy

- An employee who, for a valid reason, wishes to obtain an unpaid leave of absence not covered by other leave policies must submit a **written request to the Dean or Senior Administrative Head** for approval. The University will not refuse such a leave without valid grounds.
- The duration of an unpaid leave of absence shall **not generally exceed twelve (12) months**, except as provided under the Parental Leaves Policy.

---

## Deemed Resignation

An employee will be considered to have **submitted their resignation** on the date the leave commenced if:

1. They use the leave for reasons other than those for which it was granted, or
2. They do not return to work at the end of the leave, unless an extension has been authorized or they are prevented from returning by forces beyond their control.

---

## Return to Work

- Upon return, the University will reinstate the employee in their **previous position**.
- An employment-secured employee whose position no longer exists is covered by the Employment Security Policy.
- An employee may only return **before the end** of the unpaid leave upon written request and agreement with their immediate supervisor.

---

## Benefits During Unpaid Leave

- All benefit plans are **discontinued** for the duration of the unpaid leave, unless the employee agrees **in writing before the leave commences** to pay the total cost of any benefits to be continued.

---

## Service Credit

- An employee will **not receive credit for service** during an unpaid leave of absence for the purposes of the Employment Security Policy and the Vacation Policy.
- However, an unpaid leave of absence **does not constitute a break in continuity of service**.
