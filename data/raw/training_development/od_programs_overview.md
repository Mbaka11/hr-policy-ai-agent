# Organizational Development Developmental Programs at McGill University

## Overview

The Organizational Development (OD) department at McGill University offers a suite of structured, multi-session developmental programs designed to help employees and supervisors excel in their current roles and grow into future ones. These programs go beyond standalone workshops by providing sustained learning experiences with cohort-based engagement.

Programs are open to administrative and support staff and management staff, with specific eligibility criteria per program.

---

## Available Programs

### 1. McGill Leaders' Circle

**Audience:** Director-level leaders  
**Purpose:** A peer learning community for Directors to discuss complex leadership challenges, share best practices, and develop shared institutional perspectives.  
**Format:** Facilitated group sessions with Director-level peers.

---

### 2. Essential Skills for McGill Supervisors

**Audience:** All supervisors of administrative and support staff  
**Purpose:** Equips supervisors with foundational skills in areas such as coaching, conflict resolution, performance dialogue, and managing in a unionized environment.  
**Format:** Multi-module program covering core supervisory competencies aligned to McGill's Behavioural Competencies framework.

---

### 3. Professional Exchange Program (P.E.P.)

**Audience:** Black-identifying MPEX (Management, Professional, and Exempt) employees  
**Purpose:** Supports the professional development and career advancement of Black-identifying MPEX staff through targeted learning opportunities, mentorship, and network-building.  
**Format:** Cohort-based program with structured exchanges and facilitated discussions.

---

### 4. LEAN Six Sigma Program

**Audience:** All staff interested in process improvement  
**Purpose:** Teaches employees systematic tools and methodologies for identifying waste, reducing variability, and improving operational efficiency using the LEAN and Six Sigma frameworks.  
**Format:** Structured certification pathway with White Belt, Yellow Belt, and Green Belt levels. White Belt available as a standalone workshop.

---

### 5. Healthy Hybrid Attestation

**Audience:** All administrative and support staff  
**Purpose:** Ensures employees working in hybrid environments have the competencies and tools to work effectively across in-person and remote settings.  
**Format:** Attestation-based program confirming completion of hybrid work competency requirements.

---

### 6. Workplace Mental Health Leadership Certificate

**Audience:** Supervisors and managers  
**Purpose:** Builds leadership capacity in recognizing, responding to, and supporting mental health in the workplace.  
**Delivery partners:** Morneau Shepell and Queen's University  
**Format:** Certificate program delivered in collaboration with external partners.

---

### 7. CoachingOurselves

**Audience:** All staff  
**Purpose:** A peer-based coaching methodology where groups of employees work through structured topics together in 90-minute facilitated sessions — no external facilitator required.  
**Format:** 90-minute self-facilitated peer group sessions using CoachingOurselves topic packs.

---

### 8. French at Work Program

**Audience:** All administrative and support staff  
**Purpose:** Supports employees in developing their French language competency in a professional context, in keeping with Quebec's language requirements and McGill's bilingual operating environment.  
**Format:** Structured program with proficiency-based placement.

---

### 9. Supervisory and Management Skills Attestation

**Audience:** Supervisors and managers of administrative and support staff  
**Purpose:** Provides a structured pathway to develop and formally demonstrate core supervisory skills.  
**Format:** 6-workshop attestation series — participants receive a formal attestation upon completing all six workshops.  
**Workshops include:** areas such as managing conflict, coaching for engagement, performance dialogue, and managing in a unionized environment.

---

### 10. Leadership Development Program (LDP)

**Audience:** Management staff (MCM level and above)  
**Purpose:** A comprehensive 1-year leadership development program designed to strengthen strategic thinking, self-awareness, and leadership effectiveness for management-level employees.  
**Format:** Cohort-based, spanning 12 months with a mix of workshops, peer learning, and individual development activities.
