Our records show that you have a **hybrid work arrangement** on file with your department. As part of McGill's commitment to ensuring all staff working in hybrid environments are equipped to do so effectively, all employees with hybrid agreements are required to complete the **Healthy Hybrid Attestation** by **March 15, 2026**.

This is a mandatory completion requirement for continued hybrid work eligibility.

---

## What is the Healthy Hybrid Attestation?

The Healthy Hybrid Attestation confirms that you have the competencies, tools, and practices in place to work productively, safely, and collaboratively across both in-person and remote settings.

Completing the attestation involves:

1. Reviewing the four Healthy Hybrid competency modules (approximately 45 minutes total)
2. Confirming your home workspace meets basic ergonomic and safety standards
3. Acknowledging that you have discussed your hybrid schedule and availability expectations with your supervisor
4. Submitting your attestation through Workday

## What Happens If You Don't Complete It?

Employees who do not complete the Healthy Hybrid Attestation by March 15, 2026 **may have their hybrid work agreement temporarily suspended** until the attestation is on file, pending a conversation with their supervisor and HR.

Hybrid work is a privilege, not a right. Maintaining eligibility requires demonstrating the competencies to work in this mode effectively.

## How to Complete

1. Log in to **Workday** at http://workday.mcgill.ca
2. Go to **Learning** → **My Required Training**
3. Find **"Healthy Hybrid Attestation – 2026"** and click Start
4. Complete the 4 modules and submit the attestation form
5. Your completion will automatically update in your training record in Minerva

If the course does not appear in your Workday Learning section, contact od.hr@mcgill.ca with your employee ID.

## Ergonomic Home Workspace Checklist (Quick Reference)

As part of the attestation, you will self-certify that your home workspace meets the following minimum standards:

- [ ] Chair provides adequate lumbar support; feet can rest flat on the floor
- [ ] Monitor top is approximately at eye level (use a stand or books if needed)
- [ ] Keyboard and mouse are at elbow height with wrists in neutral position
- [ ] Adequate lighting — no glare on screen; no backlight from windows
- [ ] Internet connection is sufficiently stable for video calls
- [ ] You have a quiet space available for confidential conversations
- [ ] Printer/scanner access for documents requiring physical handling (or clear process for remote handling)

If you need ergonomic equipment to meet these standards, your department may be able to support a request. Speak with your supervisor and consult the Ergonomics Onsite, Offsite, and In-Between workshop (next session: February 17, 2026).
