Dear Colleagues,

The **2026 Merit Salary Review cycle** is approaching. This email provides an overview of the process, key timelines, and — importantly — the direct link between your **Performance Dialogue (PD) completion** and merit eligibility.

---

## What is the Merit Review?

The annual merit review is McGill's process for recognizing and rewarding employees whose performance meets or exceeds expectations. Eligible employees may receive a **salary increase effective July 1, 2026**, the start of the new fiscal year.

Merit is not automatic. It is directly informed by your Performance Dialogue results and is subject to your unit's available merit budget.

---

## Merit Eligibility Requirements

To be considered for a merit increase in 2026, **all of the following must be true**:

1. ✅ You are in a merit-eligible position (Management, MPEX, or Confidential — not covered by a collective agreement with separate grid progression)
2. ✅ You have been in your current position for at least **6 months** as of July 1, 2026 (i.e., started on or before January 1, 2026)
3. ✅ Your **2026 Performance Dialogue has been completed and signed** by the April 30, 2026 deadline
4. ✅ Your overall PD assessment is **"Meets Expectations"** or higher
5. ✅ You are not currently on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

**Employees whose PD form has not been received by HR by April 30 will not be considered for merit this cycle.** No exceptions will be made after the deadline.

---

## Merit Budget and Distribution – Key Facts for 2026

The University Merit Committee has approved the following guidelines for 2026:

- **Total merit pool:** 2.8% of eligible payroll (consistent with 2025; unchanged from prior year)
- **Guideline increases by rating:**

| Performance Rating                  | Guideline Merit Increase |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Exceptional (top ~10% of workforce) | 4.0% – 5.5%              |
| Exceeds Expectations                | 2.5% – 3.5%              |
| Meets Expectations                  | 1.0% – 2.0%              |
| Partially Meets Expectations        | 0%                       |
| Does Not Meet Expectations / On PIP | 0%                       |

These are guidelines. Final increases may vary based on the employee's position within their salary range (compa-ratio), available unit budget, and the distribution decisions made by unit heads in consultation with their HR Advisors.

- Employees **at or near the maximum of their salary range** may receive a lump-sum payment instead of a base salary increase if they are at the range ceiling.

---

## Impact of PD on Merit — What Supervisors Should Know

Supervisors are reminded that:

- Merit recommendations must be **supported by documented PD content** — vague or incomplete PD forms will be returned for revision before merit can be approved
- The Behavioural Competencies assessment is **considered alongside Individual Work Objectives** when calibrating a rating
- Ratings must be distributed consistently within units — HR Advisors will help units calibrate to avoid rating inflation
- A rating of "Exceeds Expectations" should reflect **sustained, demonstrable overperformance**, not simply the absence of problems

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I joined in January 2026 — am I eligible?**
A: Yes, if you started on or before January 1, 2026, you meet the 6-month service requirement for a July 1 effective date. However, your increase will be prorated if you are still in your probationary period.

**Q: My supervisor and I disagree on my performance rating. What can I do?**
A: Begin by having a candid conversation with your supervisor. If the disagreement cannot be resolved, you may contact your HR Advisor, who can facilitate a calibration discussion. Formal dispute resolution through the Dispute Resolution Policy is also available if needed.

**Q: What if I am on leave in April 2026?**
A: If you are on an approved leave of absence, contact hr.advisory@mcgill.ca — alternative arrangements for completing PD remotely or adjusting the timeline can be made in documented special circumstances.
