## Part 1: Before Your Leave

### Step 1 – Confirm Your Leave Dates

You indicated your expected due date is **January 18, 2026**. Based on this, your maternity leave can begin as early as **16 weeks before your due date** (i.e., September 28, 2025) or at a date you choose closer to delivery. You have confirmed you plan to start leave on **January 5, 2026**.

Please notify HR in writing at least **4 weeks before your leave start date** — you have already done this. ✅

### Step 2 – Apply for QPIP (Québec Parental Insurance Plan)

QPIP is the Quebec government program that provides income replacement during maternity leave. McGill tops up your QPIP benefit to **95% of your regular salary** for the duration of the University-paid portion of your leave.

- Apply online at: https://www.rqap.gouv.qc.ca
- You should apply **as soon as your leave begins**, or up to 4 weeks before if delivery is imminent.
- QPIP payments typically begin within 3–4 weeks of application approval.
- You will need: your Record of Employment (ROE), your SIN, and your expected/actual delivery date.

**McGill Top-Up:** Once QPIP begins paying, McGill's payroll will calculate the difference between your QPIP benefit and 95% of your regular salary and pay that difference directly. You do not need to apply separately — your HR Advisor will coordinate with payroll.

### Step 3 – Record of Employment (ROE)

McGill will issue your ROE automatically within **5 business days** of your last paid day. You do not need to request it.

### Step 4 – Benefits During Leave

Your benefits (health, dental, life insurance) **remain active** throughout your maternity leave. Premiums continue to be deducted from your McGill top-up payments. If top-up ceases before you return to work, you will receive an invoice for premiums — contact benefits.hr@mcgill.ca in that scenario.

## Part 2: Your Leave Entitlements – Summary

| Leave Type                                 | Duration                         | Pay                                                         |
| ------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Maternity Leave (McGill policy)            | 20 weeks                         | 95% of salary (McGill top-up + QPIP)                        |

## Part 3: During Your Leave

- You are not required to check email or be available for work during your leave.
- If you wish to receive general staff communications (e.g., training calendar, team updates), please let Marc know your preference — this is entirely optional.
- If your personal contact information changes, please update it in Workday so HR can reach you if needed.
- **Vacation accrual:** You continue to accrue vacation credits during your maternity leave under the standard Reference Year (June 1 – May 31).

---

## Part 4: Returning to Work

### Notice of Return

Please give at least **4 weeks' written notice** of your return date. You can email me directly or update your return date in Workday.

### Flexible Return Options

If you would like to return on a **part-time basis** for a transitional period, this may be possible depending on operational needs. Discuss this with Marc and contact me to formalize any modified schedule in writing. Note: if using extended parental leave in part-time mode, minimum 14 hours/week applies.

### Nursing/Lactation

McGill has designated lactation spaces on campus. Contact Campus Wellness at wellness@mcgill.ca for the list of rooms closest to your building.
