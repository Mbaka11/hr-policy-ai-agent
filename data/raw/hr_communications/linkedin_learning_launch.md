Dear Colleagues,

We are thrilled to announce that **LinkedIn Learning is now available at no cost** to all Administrative and Support Staff at McGill University!

LinkedIn Learning gives you instant access to **over 21,000 on-demand video courses** taught by industry experts, covering topics from leadership and communication to data analysis, project management, coding, and creative design.

---

## Why LinkedIn Learning?

Over the past two years, feedback from staff surveys has consistently highlighted a desire for **flexible, self-paced learning options** that can be fit into busy schedules. LinkedIn Learning answers that call:

- Learn at your own pace — pause, rewind, and revisit content any time
- Access courses on desktop, tablet, or mobile
- Choose from short 5-minute micro-lessons or comprehensive multi-hour courses
- Receive AI-powered course recommendations based on your role and interests
- Earn digital certificates of completion to add to your professional profile

In our pilot with 120 staff members in Winter 2025, **89% of participants rated the platform as useful or very useful**, and the average learning time was 2.4 hours per month.

---

## Who Can Access?

All **Administrative and Support Staff**, including:

- Management (M) and MPEX employees
- Confidential (C) employees
- Non-unionized administrative staff

_Note: Faculty and graduate students are not included in this license tier. Academic staff interested in access should contact their Faculty HR._

---

## How to Set Up Your Account

**Step 1:** Go to the McGill LinkedIn Learning portal:
http://lnkd.in/eKvf67m

**Step 2:** Click **"Sign in with your organization"** and enter your McGill email address.

**Step 3:** You will be redirected to McGill's Single Sign-On (SSO) — log in with your McGill credentials (same as Workday, Minerva, etc.).

**Step 4:** You're in! No additional registration or personal LinkedIn account required.

_If you already have a personal LinkedIn account, you will be given the option to link it (to save your learning history to your profile) or keep it separate. This choice is entirely yours._

## Tracking Your Learning

Your LinkedIn Learning activity is tracked within the platform and a summary can be downloaded directly. You can also cite completed courses in your **Individual Development Action Plan** and during your Performance Dialogue session as evidence of development activity.
