## The Policy

McGill's **Policy on Harassment and Discrimination Prohibited by Law** (formerly "Policy against Harassment and Discrimination") applies to:

- All university employees (academic and non-academic, full-time and part-time)
- All unionized and non-unionized staff
- Senior administrators
- In some circumstances, contractors, suppliers, and visitors acting in a McGill capacity

The policy prohibits any conduct that constitutes **harassment or discrimination** based on grounds protected under the Quebec _Charter of Human Rights and Freedoms_ and the Canadian _Human Rights Act_, including:

- Race, colour, national or ethnic origin
- Religion or creed
- Sex (including pregnancy and gender identity/expression)
- Sexual orientation
- Age
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Civil or family status
- Political convictions

## What Counts as Harassment?

Harassment is defined as **vexatious conduct** (a single serious incident or repeated conduct) that is known or which ought reasonably to be known to be unwanted, and that adversely affects the dignity of a person.

Common examples include:

- Repeated demeaning comments about a colleague's work, appearance, or personal characteristics
- Exclusion from meetings, communications, or professional opportunities on the basis of a protected ground
- Unwanted physical contact
- Threats, intimidation, or aggressive behaviour
- Sharing or displaying offensive material (electronic or physical)
- Sexual comments, advances, or requests that are unwelcome

**Important:** A single incident can constitute harassment if it is sufficiently serious (for example, a physical assault or explicit threat).

### What Harassment is NOT

Not every difficult interaction is harassment. Reasonable management actions — such as assigning tasks, giving performance feedback, scheduling, or implementing disciplinary measures — do not constitute harassment, even if the employee disagrees with the decision.

## What to Do If You Experience or Witness Harassment

**If you feel safe doing so:**
Inform the person that their behaviour is unwelcome and ask them to stop.

**If you are not comfortable raising it directly, or if the behaviour continues:**

1. **Document the incidents** — dates, times, location, what was said or done, who was present
2. **Contact your HR Advisor** — hr.advisory@mcgill.ca | 514-398-4835. Your HR Advisor provides confidential guidance on your options.
3. **Contact the Office for Mediation and Reporting (OMR)** — for formal complaints and mediation services. The OMR handles complaints under this policy at all stages including investigation.
   - Website: https://www.mcgill.ca/omr
   - Email: omr@mcgill.ca
   - Phone: 514-398-2600

**If you witness harassment involving someone else:**
Bystander intervention can be powerful. If it is safe to do so, you may:

- Address the behaviour in the moment ("That comment wasn't okay")
- Check in with the affected person after
- Encourage them to report the behaviour and offer to accompany them
- Report what you witnessed to HR or the OMR (you do not need the other person's permission to report what you observed)

## Key Facts from 2024–2025

In the 2024–2025 academic year, the OMR received **47 formal complaints** related to harassment and discrimination. Of those:

- 31% were resolved through informal mediation
- 42% proceeded to a formal investigation
- 18% were withdrawn by the complainant
- 9% were deemed outside the scope of the policy after preliminary review

The most common grounds cited: workplace bullying (behaviour-based harassment, not linked to a protected ground — handled separately), sex/gender, and disability.

The average time to resolution for a formal complaint was **18 weeks** (down from 23 weeks in 2023–2024).

## Confidentiality

Complaints are handled with the highest degree of confidentiality. Only those who need to know for the purpose of investigating or resolving a complaint will be informed. Retaliation against anyone who files a complaint in good faith, participates in an investigation, or supports a complainant is **strictly prohibited** and is itself a violation of the policy.
