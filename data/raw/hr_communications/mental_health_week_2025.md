Dear Colleagues,

This week, May 5–9, 2025, is **Mental Health Week** in Canada, and McGill's My Healthy Workplace is marking the occasion with a full week of programming, resources, and community-building activities.

This year's national theme is **"Healing Together"**, recognizing that we don't face mental health challenges alone — and that our workplace community plays a real role in recovery and resilience.

---

## Why This Week Matters at McGill

In the 2024 McGill Staff Wellbeing Survey (n = 1,847 respondents):

- **61%** of staff reported experiencing moderate to high levels of work-related stress in the past 6 months
- **34%** said they had experienced symptoms of burnout at some point during the academic year
- **Only 47%** said they felt comfortable speaking with their supervisor about mental health concerns
- **78%** said they would use workplace mental health resources if they knew how to access them

These numbers tell us there is real need — and real opportunity — to do better together.

## Year-Round Resources

You don't have to wait for Mental Health Week to get support.

**Employee and Family Assistance Program (EFAP)**

- Free, confidential counselling for you and your immediate family members
- Up to **8 sessions per issue** per year, at no cost
- Available 24/7 by phone: 1-800-663-1142
- Online access: https://www.mcgill.ca/hr/benefits/eap
- Services in English, French, and 170+ other languages

**Dialogue (Virtual Mental Health Care)**

- Access a therapist or psychologist through the Dialogue app within 5 business days
- Available to all benefits-eligible employees and their covered dependents
- Register: https://www.dialogue.co (use your McGill employee ID)

**My Healthy Workplace Resource Library**
Guides on stress, sleep, resilience, grief, return-to-work, and more:
https://www.mcgill.ca/myhealthyworkplace

## A Note from HR

Mental health challenges are common, real, and treatable. Seeking support is not a sign of weakness — it is a sign of self-awareness, which is one of McGill's core Behavioural Competencies for a reason.

If you are in a leadership role, we encourage you to share this email with your team, check in with your people this week, and model openness. A simple "How are you, really?" can make a difference.

If you or someone you know is in crisis, please contact:

- **Crisis line (Quebec):** 1-866-APPELLE (277-3553)
- **McGill Security (after hours):** 514-398-3000

Take care of yourselves and each other.
