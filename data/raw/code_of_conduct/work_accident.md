# Work Accidents Policy

## Scope

This policy applies to:

- All **regular non-academic, non-unionized staff**
- A "regular employee" is one who has completed the probationary period

---

## Salary and Compensation

### During First 52 Weeks of Disability

- The University pays:
  - Employee’s **regular salary**
  - Minus standard deductions

- Condition:
  - Employee must be eligible for compensation under the **CSST**

- CSST indemnity:
  - Paid to the **University**, not directly to the employee

---

### After 52 Weeks

- If the employee is still unable to work:
  - Payments are made **directly by the CSST to the employee**

---

## Long-Term Disability

- Around the **10th month of disability**:
  - Employee must complete eligibility forms for long-term disability benefits

---

## Medical Examination

- The University may require:
  - Examination by a doctor chosen by the University

- Employee rights:
  - Must receive a copy of the medical report within **5 working days**

---

## Return to Work

### Reinstatement

- Employee is reinstated to:
  - Their **original position**, OR
  - If unavailable:
    - A position with equivalent rights

---

### Salary Upon Return

- Employee receives:
  - Their previous salary
  - Plus any salary increases that occurred during leave
  - Subject to eligibility criteria

---

### Permanent Disability Case

If the employee cannot return to their previous role:

- They are placed in another suitable position
- Based on:
  - Health condition
  - Available roles
- No posting process required

---

## Medical Leave After Return

- If employee needs time off for:
  - Treatments
  - Medical exams
  - Rehabilitation

→ Leave is granted **without loss of salary**

---

## Notes

- Policy is governed by the **Act respecting Industrial Accidents and Occupational Diseases**
- Effective date: April 30, 2000
