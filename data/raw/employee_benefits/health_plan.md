# Supplemental Health Plan

## Overview

The McGill Supplemental Health Plan provides coverage for medical items, services, and procedures **not covered by the provincial healthcare plan**.

To qualify:

- Employees and eligible dependents must be covered under a **provincial health plan**
- Non-residents or returning residents may be subject to a **3-month waiting period** for Quebec Medicare (RAMQ) :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## Eligibility Requirements

- Must qualify for provincial healthcare coverage
- Applies to:
  - Employee
  - Spouse
  - Eligible children

---

## Mandatory Participation

Under Quebec law:

- Employees under age 65 **must be covered by a group health insurance plan** (at minimum for drug coverage)
- Employees may opt out **only if they provide proof of alternative coverage** :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## Opting Out

Employees may opt out of the plan if:

- They are covered under another supplemental health plan (e.g., through a spouse)

---

## Out-of-Pocket Maximum

The out-of-pocket amount is the portion paid by the employee when services are reimbursed at **75% or 80%**.

### Once the maximum is reached:

- All eligible expenses are reimbursed at **100%** for the remainder of the year

### Limits

| Coverage Type                | Maximum         |
| ---------------------------- | --------------- |
| Single                       | $400/year       |
| Family                       | $800/year total |
| Per individual (family plan) | $400            |

- Benefit year: **January 1 – December 31** :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## Coverage Details

The plan covers **reasonable and customary charges**, defined as typical costs for similar services in the same area.

### Covered Services

- Semi-private hospital accommodations (Canada)
- Prescription drugs (80%)
- Health practitioner services (80%)
- Medical supplies and services (75%)
- Out-of-province medical services (100%)
- Personalized medicine (100%)

---

### Semi-Private Hospital Coverage

- Covers **100% of the difference** between:
  - Standard ward rate (provincial plan)
  - Semi-private room rate

- Includes outpatient hospital services :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

---

## What Is Not Covered

The plan does **not cover** certain services and expenses, including:

### Examples of Non-Covered Items

- Costs exceeding reasonable and customary charges
- Equipment such as:
  - Orthopedic mattresses
  - Exercise equipment
  - Air purifiers
- Custodial care
- Transportation (except emergency ambulance)
- Services from non-licensed practitioners
- Eyeglasses, contact lenses, and laser eye surgery
- Cosmetic or non-medically necessary treatments
- Experimental procedures
- Brand-name drugs when a generic exists
- Private doctor fees (unless specified)
- Long-term care stays
- Diagnostic tests performed outside approved settings
- Vaccination administration costs

### Exclusions Related to Situations

- War, riots, or criminal activity
- Workplace injury covered by compensation laws
- Government-covered services
- Travel for health purposes

---

## When Coverage Ends

Coverage will terminate if the employee:

- Leaves the university before retirement
- No longer meets eligibility requirements
- Opts out (age 65+ only)
- Loses provincial health coverage
- Moves outside Canada
- Fails to pay premiums :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

---

## Dependent Coverage Termination

- Dependent coverage ends when employee coverage ends
- Individual dependent coverage ends if eligibility is lost (e.g., marriage, full-time employment)

---

## Special Case: Death Before Retirement

If an employee dies before age 65:

- Spouse and eligible children may continue coverage for up to **3 months**
- The university covers both employee and employer contributions during this period :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
