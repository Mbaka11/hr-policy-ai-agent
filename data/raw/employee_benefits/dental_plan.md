# Dental Plan

## Overview

The McGill Dental Plan covers a wide range of dental services, from routine check-ups to advanced procedures such as root canals.

- Participation is **optional**
- Coverage applies to employees and eligible dependents :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## Optional Participation

- Employees may choose not to enroll at the time of hire
- Re-enrollment is possible:
  - Every **3 years (January)**
  - Must still meet eligibility criteria and be actively working :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## Dental Fee Guide

- Reimbursements are based on the **Quebec Dental Fee Guide**
- If a dentist charges more than the recommended fee:
  - The employee must pay the difference :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## Annual Maximum

- Maximum reimbursement:
  - **$2,000 per insured person per year**
- Benefit year:
  - **January 1 – December 31** :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

---

## Coverage Breakdown

### Reimbursement Rates

| Category                 | Coverage |
| ------------------------ | -------- |
| Preventive               | 100%     |
| Restorative              | 80%      |
| Endodontic / Periodontal | 80%      |
| Major Services           | 70%      |
| Orthodontics             | 50%      |

---

### Preventive Services (100%)

#### Frequency Rules

- **Twice per year (every 5 months):**
  - Bitewing radiographs
  - Oral hygiene instruction
  - Cleaning (prophylaxis)
  - Recall exams
  - Fluoride (under 16)

- **Once every 2 years:**
  - Complete oral exam
  - Full radiographs or panoramic x-rays

- **As required:**
  - Biopsy
  - Cytology tests
  - Emergency exams
  - Radiographs for diagnosis
  - Specialist consultation :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

---

## What Is Not Covered

The plan does **not cover**:

### General Exclusions

- Services already covered by another plan
- Services exceeding fee guide limits
- Cosmetic or non-medically necessary treatments
- Full mouth reconstructions and TMJ treatments
- Missed appointments or administrative fees
- Replacement of lost or stolen dentures

---

### Specific Exclusions

- Dental care related to:
  - War, riots, or criminal activity
  - Workplace injuries covered by compensation laws
- Implants (only reimbursed up to the cost of cheaper alternatives)
- Mouthguards or sport-related devices
- Services not clinically required
- Periodontal appliances for children under 16 (in most cases)
- Diagnostic or hygiene add-ons like desensitization :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

---

## Treatment Plans

### Pre-Approval Requirement

- If treatment exceeds **$400**:
  - A treatment plan should be submitted to the insurer before treatment

### Purpose

- Confirms coverage eligibility
- Avoids unexpected out-of-pocket costs :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

---

### Alternate Treatment Rule

- If multiple treatment options exist:
  - Reimbursement is based on the **least expensive acceptable option** :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

---

## When Coverage Ends

Coverage terminates if the employee:

- Leaves the university before retirement
- No longer meets eligibility requirements
- Opts out of the plan
- Moves outside Canada
- Fails to pay premiums :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

---

## Dependent Coverage

- Ends when employee coverage ends
- Also ends if dependent becomes ineligible (e.g., marriage, full-time employment)

---

## Post-Termination Coverage

- Ongoing treatments may still be reimbursed:
  - If expenses occur within **31 days after departure** :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

---

## Disability Case

- Coverage continues during long-term disability:
  - If the employee continues to pay their share of costs :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

---

## Death of Employee

If the employee dies before age 65:

- Spouse and dependents can continue coverage for:
  - Up to **3 months**
- The university pays both employer and employee contributions :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
