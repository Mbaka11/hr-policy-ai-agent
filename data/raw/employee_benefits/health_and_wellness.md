# Health and Well-Being Resources

- Financial wellness
- General health

---

# Financial Wellness

## Overview

Financial well-being is essential for overall health. Financial stress may lead to:

- Sleep problems
- Reduced productivity
- Mental health issues

Employees are encouraged to:

- Assess their financial situation
- Educate themselves
- Set financial goals

---

## Support Resources

### Employee Assistance Program (EFAP)

- Provides **free and confidential support** for financial stress

---

## Learning Programs

### Personal Finance Course

- Free course offered by McGill (Desautels Faculty)
- Available in English and French
- Offered multiple times per year
- Certification provided upon completion

---

### TELUS Health Program

- Self-directed program: _Achieving Financial Wellbeing_
- Focus:
  - Budgeting
  - Money management habits
  - Financial literacy

---

### Sun Life Financial Webinars

- Topics include:
  - Investing
  - Retirement planning
  - Money management

# Mental Health

## Definition

Mental health is defined as a state of well-being that enables individuals to:

- Cope with stress
- Work productively
- Build relationships
- Contribute to their community

---

## Self-Assessment Tools

- Mental Health Meter
- Stress Index
- Work-Life Balance Quiz

---

## Support Resources

- Anxiety Canada (self-help tools)
- Canadian Mental Health Association
- MindShift app (CBT-based tools)
- Multicultural Mental Health Resource Centre

---

## Employee Assistance Program (EFAP)

- Confidential counselling
- Available to employees and dependents
- Covers:
  - Mental health support
  - Personal issues

# Physical Well-Being

## Activity Guidelines

- Adults (18–65):
  - At least **150 minutes of moderate to vigorous activity per week**

---

## Exercise Options

- Walking and running programs
- Home workouts
- Office exercises
- Fitness programs (online resources)

---

## Sleep

- Recommended:
  - **7–9 hours per night**
- Sleep quality depends on individual patterns

---

## Sun Safety

- Protection from UV exposure is important to reduce cancer risk

---

## Notes

- Information provided is general guidance
