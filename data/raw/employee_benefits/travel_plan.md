# Emergency Travel Assistance

## Overview

Emergency Travel Assistance provides coverage for **emergency medical expenses** when traveling outside your province of residence.

- Applies to employees and eligible dependents
- Requires active provincial health coverage (e.g., RAMQ) :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## Coverage Summary

- Coverage applies to:
  - Sudden, unexpected injuries
  - New medical conditions
  - Previously diagnosed conditions that are **medically stable**

- Lifetime maximum:
  - **$5,000,000 per person**

- Coverage applies while traveling outside the province :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Definition of Medical Emergency

A medical emergency is:

- A sudden, unforeseen injury or condition
- A previously diagnosed condition that was stable before departure

A medical emergency ends when:

- The patient is medically able to return home :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

---

## Definition of Medically Stable

A condition is considered stable if, in the **90 days before departure**, the individual has not:

- Experienced new symptoms
- Had worsening symptoms
- Changed treatment or medication (except routine adjustments)
- Been hospitalized for the condition :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

---

## Coverage Limits

### Trip Duration

- Up to **90 days per trip** :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

---

### Business Travel (AIG)

- Maximum:
  - **$1,000,000 per person**
- Duration:
  - Up to **180 days per trip**
- Reduced to $500,000 after age 75 :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

---

## Covered Services

### Medical Services

- Hospitalization
- Physician services
- X-rays and lab tests
- Medication (hospital-administered)
- Outpatient hospital services

---

### Transportation

- Emergency transport to nearest medical facility
- Return transport to home province
- Transport of family members
- Return of dependent children
- Return of vehicle
- Repatriation of remains :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

---

## What Is Not Covered

### General Exclusions

- Non-emergency services
- Routine medical visits or tests
- Follow-up care for existing conditions
- Travel-related non-medical expenses (e.g., lost luggage)

---

### Medical Restrictions

- Ongoing treatment of pre-existing conditions
- Pregnancy-related care after the **32nd week**
- Childbirth expenses after the 32nd week

---

### Administrative Conditions

- Services when provincial coverage is inactive
- Failure to contact emergency provider
- Expenses below $200 (for direct processing) :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

---

## RAMQ Restrictions

### Temporary Residents

- Maximum absence:
  - **21 consecutive days**
- Beyond this:
  - No provincial or travel coverage

---

### Permanent Residents

- Maximum absence:
  - **183 days**
- Must notify RAMQ if exceeding this period :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

---

## Claims Process

1. Contact emergency provider (Global Excel Management)
2. Keep all receipts and itemized bills
3. Submit claim to:
   - RAMQ first
   - Then Manulife for remaining balance :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

---

## Coverage Restrictions

- Only **emergency medical services** are covered
- Coverage ends if:
  - Provincial coverage ends
  - Travel conditions violate policy rules :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

---

## Special Cases

### Pregnancy

- Covered only before the **32nd week**
- Only emergency-related treatment is covered

---

### Death

- Return of remains is covered
- Transportation assistance is included :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
