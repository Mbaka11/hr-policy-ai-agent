# Vacation Policy

## 1. Scope

This policy applies to all regular full-time and part-time non-academic staff.

---

## 2. Policy

### 2.1 Accrual for Less Than One Year of Service

Employees with less than one (1) year of service as of May 31 will be granted paid vacation at the rate of:

- **1/4 week per complete month of service**

---

### 2.2 Accrual for One Year or More of Service

Employees with one (1) or more years of service as of May 31 will be granted paid vacation according to the following schedule:

| Years of Service  | Vacation Entitlement |
| ----------------- | -------------------- |
| 1 year < 3 years  | 3 weeks              |
| 3 years < 7 years | 4 weeks              |
| 7 years or more   | 5 weeks              |

---

### 2.3 Part-Time and Sessional Staff

Sessional and part-time staff are granted paid vacation based on the same rules as above, but **pro-rated**.

---

### 2.4 Legacy Policy (Pre-1978 Employees)

Employees employed as of June 1, 1978 and classified as M-3, M-4, or M-5 may be granted vacation under the following schedule **if more advantageous**:

| Years of Service    | Vacation Entitlement |
| ------------------- | -------------------- |
| 10 years < 25 years | 5 weeks              |
| 25 years or more    | 6 weeks              |

---

## 3. General Rules

### 3.1 Non-Standard Work Schedules

For employees working schedules other than a standard five-day week:

- Vacation is calculated based on the **total number of hours worked in a normal week**

---

### 3.2 Service Credit Rule

Employees who begin work **before the 16th day of a month** receive credit for a **full month of service** for vacation calculation.

---

## 4. Administrative Procedures

### 4.1 Reference Year

- Vacation reference year: **June 1 to May 31**
- Vacation earned during a reference year:
  - Becomes available **only after the end of that year**
  - Cannot be used before the next reference period

---

### 4.2 Mandatory Usage (Quebec Labour Law)

- Vacation must be taken within **12 months** after the reference year
- It **cannot be replaced by financial compensation** (except on termination)

---

## 5. Scheduling of Vacations

### 5.1 Responsibility

- Vacation scheduling is managed by the **Department Head**
- Must balance:
  - Employee preferences
  - Service needs
  - Operational efficiency

---

### 5.2 Progressive Return to Work

Employees may request vacation during a progressive return-to-work period, subject to standard scheduling rules.

---

### 5.3 Vacation Splitting and Notice

- Vacation may be split into more than two periods **with employer approval**
- Employees must be informed of vacation dates at least:
  - **4 weeks in advance**

---

## 6. Termination of Service

### 6.1 Compensation for Unused Vacation

Upon termination, employees receive a **compensatory indemnity** for unused vacation.

---

### 6.2 Calculation of Compensation

Compensation includes:

- (a) Vacation earned in the previous reference year but not taken
- (b) Vacation earned in the current reference year (even if not yet vested)

---

### 6.3 Final Month Credit Rule

Employees terminating after the **15th day of a month** receive credit for a **full month of service**.

---

### 6.4 Payment

- Compensation is added to the employee’s **final paycheck**
